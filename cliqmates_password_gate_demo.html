Turning Ideas into Reality

Built by someone who knows people

I arrived in the UK in 2005, with no connections, capital, or comfort.

What I did have was ambition. The drive that turns bartenders into business owners, and dreamers into founders.

I built and sold multiple hospitality businesses from scratch, no investors, no shortcuts.

People-first thinking and a solid instinct for what works, is proof that if you understand people, you can build something meaningful, impactful, and profitable.

But even in those buzzing venues, I saw something change. People were out, but not really present. The energy had shifted. Conversations faded. Screens took over.

That's when an idea started - what if there was an app for that? Tech that bridges the gap to reconnection.

Six years later, that idea has a name: CliQmates

Decline of Human Connection

In a world that's more "connected" than ever, real-world connections are disappearing. People long for genuine interaction but don't know how to find it, or worse, have lost the confidence to initiate it. This is the gap we're aiming to fill with CliQmates: real-world connections, when and where they matter most.

🏙

1. Disconnected in the Crowd

Rapid urban relocation is breaking support systems once held by families and communities. Over 60% of urban Indians say they don't have anyone to confide in. Busy cities offer opportunities, but lasting friendships are harder than ever to form.

📷

2. Screens Replaced Presence

Social media is everywhere - but so is emotional disconnection. Indian teens and young adults who spend excessive time online are up to 22x more likely to report anxiety symptoms. Real-life interaction is being replaced with scrolling, noise, and curated perfection.

💥

3. The Cost of Isolation

People want real connection but digital tools often amplify the problem. The WHO now classifies loneliness as a global epidemic, tied to serious health risks. In India, mental health helplines report loneliness among the top three reasons for distress calls.

📈

Loneliness is fuelling a global mental health crisis

Loneliness is linked to depression in 70% of Indian adults. Over 50% of adults report feeling emotionally disconnected from others. Young adults seeking meaningful friendships is at an all-time high, yet the tools to find them remain broken.
